Three Design Principles

Emily Wall

Visual Hierarchy

Scott's Cheap Flights
scottscheapflights.com

Scott's Cheap Flights does a great job with visual hierarchy. The main value proposition, "Never overpay for flights again," is in big, bold font that the reader sees first. After that, the reader's eye is naturally drawn to the orange call-to-action buttons that contrast with the rest of the page. The cute illustration of a vacationer on an exotic beach is the next thing that gets noticed. It is in lighter colors so that it doesn't compete with the aforementioned items. The final detail that the reader notices is the smaller text about saving up to 90% on flights. The nice visual hierarchy of the page probably increases the conversion rate for Scott's Cheap Flights.

PARC: Repetition

Six Sisters' Stuff
sixsistersstuff.com

While there are other good design principles in play on this page, the one that stands out to me is repetition. Each recipe is displayed in a box of the same size following the same pattern. First, there is a delicious-looking picture of the recipe. The recipe title comes next. Underneath the title is a little clock icon with the estimated time that the recipe takes. By repeating the same look and feel for each recipe, the universe feels in balance and the sight maintains its consistency.

White Space and Clean Design

Healthline
healthline.com

This mobile view of Healthline is very simple and uncluttered. There is a lot of white space between each topic and there are just three topics visible at a time. This provides visual "breathing space" and keeps the reader from being overwhelmed.
