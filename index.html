Frontend Design Philosophy

The most important thing in frontend web design isn't a thing at all; it's a person! It's the end user! I understand the importance of thinking about psychology and marketing before ever writing a line of code. I believe in designing interfaces that meet the needs and wants of the customer while simultaneously looking great.

One of the best ways to create a user-friendly design that looks great is to remember the principle of visual hierarchy. Whatever the user should see or do first needs to stand out. I also believe it's important to avoid overwhelming the user with too much content on the screen. Keep it clean. Keep it simple. Keep the important things obvious.

Work Examples

Here are a few examples of my work from class assignments.

Menu Planner & Shopping List ↗

For this assignment, I had to come up with an app that used a third-party API and local storage. I decided to make a menu planner app that would tailor the recipe results to how busy the user planned on being on a particular day. For example, if the user clicks that they are busy, the recipe results that are returned are for recipes that take under 30 minutes to prepare. The user's selections are then saved in local storage.

Chamber of Commerce Site ↗

The goal of this assignment was to design a fake chamber of commerce website for my city of residence. There were many specific requirements for the assignment, such as using a weather API, designing the shortcut icon, creating a form, embedding a map, and so on.

Creative Ad ↗

This assignment was from my Visual Media class. The brief for this assignment was to come up with a creative ad for a computer-generated household object and a computer-generated demographic. My computer-generated object was a desk organizer and my demographic was married females between the ages of 35-44 who would see the ad in a magazine. I designed the ad in Adobe Photoshop.

Icon Set ↗

This assignment was also from my Visual Media class. I had to use Adobe Illustrator to design a set of four icons based around a common theme. The theme that I came up with was "mom nag icons." One of my icons is a reminder to flush the toilet, one is a reminder to wash your hands, one is a reminder to hang up wet towels, and one is a reminder to rinse dirty dishes. I used repeating shapes and colors to make the set look cohesive.
